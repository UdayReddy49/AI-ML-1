The fruit is typically in season in the Northern Hemisphere from September to February,[3] and in the Southern Hemisphere from March to May. As intact sarcotestas or juice, pomegranates are used in baking, cooking, juice blends, meal garnishes, smoothies, and alcoholic beverages, such as cocktails and wine.Today, it is widely cultivated throughout the Middle East and Caucasus region, north and tropical Africa,the Indian subcontinent, Central Asia, the drier parts of southeast Asia, and parts of the Mediterranean
